# APPENDIX: CONCORDANCE OF RESTORED NAMES AND TERMS

## Restored Name Glossary

**Yahuah** (יָהוָה)
- Paleo-Hebrew: Yod-Hey-Uau-Hey
- Meaning: "I AM that I AM" / Self-Existent One
- Notes: The true Name of the Most High, often replaced with "LORD" or "God" in modern texts

**Yahusha** (יָהוּשַׁע)
- Paleo-Hebrew: Yah + Yasha
- Meaning: "Yahuah is Salvation"
- Notes: True Name of the Messiah; not "Jesus," which is a Greek transliteration of a Latinized name

**Yah** (יָהּ)
- Meaning: "I AM" / "He Who Is"
- Notes: Shortened form of Yahuah, used in praise (e.g., HalleluYah); a valid and reverent contraction

**Elohiym / Alohiym** (אֱלֹהִים)
- Meaning: "Mighty Ones"
- Notes: Both transliterations are valid; some believe "Alohiym" preserves the Aleph (א) and avoids Greek vowel inversion. "God" is excluded due to its potential link to Gadre'el

**Ruach Ha'Qodesh** (רוּחַ הַקֹּדֶשׁ)
- Meaning: "Set-Apart Breath"
- Notes: Often mistranslated as "Holy Spirit"; "holy" is a Greco-Roman term with pagan roots. "Set-Apart" preserves the original intent of qodesh (קדש)

**El Elyon** (אֵל עֶלְיוֹן)
- Meaning: "Most High Mighty One"
- Notes: A title of Yahuah emphasizing supremacy

**Shamayim** (שָׁמַיִם)
- Meaning: "Heavens" / "Sky realms"
- Notes: Often mistranslated as "heaven" (singular); dual form implies layered realms

## Clarification Note on "Elohiym" vs "Alohiym"

While both are transliterations of אֱלֹהִים, some believe the vowel shift from "A" to "E" is part of a linguistic inversion—a subtle distortion of the original frequency. "Alohiym" emphasizes the Aleph (א), the first letter of the Hebrew alphabet, symbolizing origin, strength, and the Father in Paleo-Hebrew thought. For this reason, both forms are included, but "Alohiym" may be preferred by those seeking to restore the earliest phonetic integrity.
