# CHAPTER 18: THE NAME THAT WAS ERASED

"LORD" appears over 6,800 times in the King James Old Testament. But it's not a translation - it's a replacement. The Hebrew text says "Yahuah" (or YHWH in the consonantal form). The translators chose to substitute the name with a title.

## The Documentary Evidence

**The Hebrew Manuscripts**:
The Tetragrammaton (YHWH - יהוה) appears approximately 6,828 times in the Hebrew Masoretic Text. This is the most frequent proper noun in the Hebrew scriptures.

The Dead Sea Scrolls confirm this. In the Great Isaiah Scroll (1QIsa), dated to approximately 125 BCE, the divine name appears in Paleo-Hebrew script - even when the surrounding text is in Aramaic square script. The scribes went out of their way to preserve the name distinctly.

**The Septuagint (LXX) Evidence**:
The earliest Greek translations of the Hebrew scriptures preserved the Tetragrammaton. Papyrus Fouad 266, dated to the 1st century BCE, contains portions of Deuteronomy with YHWH written in Hebrew letters within the Greek text.

The substitution of "Kyrios" (Lord) for YHWH came later - after Christianity was institutionalized. Early manuscripts show the name was there. Later copies show it replaced.

**The Translators' Acknowledgment**:
The King James Version translators admitted what they did. In the preface to the 1611 edition, they explained their choice to use "LORD" (in small capitals) to indicate where the divine name appeared in Hebrew.

But an acknowledgment doesn't undo the effect. Millions of readers for centuries have been disconnected from the actual name of their Creator.

## The Scriptural Commands

And the name matters. Shemoth (Exodus) 3:15 could not be clearer:

"And Elohiym said moreover unto Mosheh, Thus shall you say unto the children of Yashar'el, Yahuah Elohiym of your fathers, the Elohiym of Avraham, the Elohiym of Yitschaq, and the Elohiym of Ya'aqov, has sent me unto you: **this is my name forever**, and this is **my memorial unto all generations**."

Not a name for one era. Forever. Not a title - a name. Not subject to human revision - a memorial for all generations.

**Yesha'yahu (Isaiah) 42:8** declares: "I am Yahuah: that is my name: and my glory will I not give to another, neither my praise to graven images."

**Tehilliym (Psalm) 68:4**: "Sing unto Elohiym, sing praises to his name: extol him that rideth upon the heavens by his name Yah, and rejoice before him."

The name isn't just identification. It's identity. It's covenant. It's power. That's why it was targeted.

## The Messiah's Name

The same happened with the Messiah's name.

**Yahusha** (יהושע). It means "Yahuah is salvation." It contains the divine name. It declares the source of redemption.

This is why Ma'asiym (Acts) 4:12 matters so deeply: "Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is no other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved."

The name matters. The specific name. Not a generic title - the name.

**The Transliteration Journey**:
- Hebrew: **Yahusha** (יהושע) or **Yeshua** (ישוע) - meaning "Yahuah is salvation"
- Greek: **Iesous** (Ἰησοῦς) - an attempt to transliterate into Greek phonetics
- Latin: **Iesus** - Latin adaptation
- English: **Jesus** - the J didn't exist until the 16th century; before that, it was Iesus

By the time it arrives in English, the meaning is gone. The connection to Yahuah is severed. "Jesus" doesn't mean anything in English. It's just a label without content.

**The Letter J**:
The letter "J" didn't exist in English until approximately 1524. Before that, the name was written "Iesus" and pronounced with a Y sound. The modern pronunciation "JEE-zus" is only about 500 years old.

The original name - Yahusha - has been spoken for over 2,000 years. The substituted name is relatively recent.

## Does Pronunciation Matter?

Some argue that names are arbitrary. That the Creator knows who is being called regardless of the label used. That sincerity matters more than pronunciation.

There's truth in this. The heart behind the prayer matters. But there's also truth in precision:

**Yo'el (Joel) 2:32** prophesies: "And it shall come to pass, that whosoever shall call on **the name** of Yahuah shall be delivered."

**Yahuchanon (John) 17:6** records Yahusha's own words: "I have manifested **your name** unto the men which you gave me out of the world."

He didn't manifest a title. He manifested the name.

**The Power of Names**:
When someone deliberately changes your name, it's an act of power. It's saying they get to define you. Consider:

# CHAPTER 19: THE DUAL CRUCIFIXION - A THEORY TO CONSIDER

The crucifixion is the central event of Christianity. The sacrifice that atones. The death that brings life. But what if even this event has layers that haven't been examined?

This is a theory. Not doctrine. Not certainty. But a pattern worth considering.

## The Scene at Golgotha

First, understand what Golgotha actually was. Not a sacred hill. Not a dramatic mountaintop. A roadside. A public thoroughfare where merchants walked by, children played, and life continued while men hung dying on stakes.

**The Roman Practice**:
Romans didn't crucify just three people at a time. Crucifixion was mass execution - dozens, sometimes hundreds, dying along the roads as warnings to the population.

- After the Spartacus rebellion (71 BCE), 6,000 slaves were crucified along the Appian Way
- Josephus records mass crucifixions during the siege of Jerusalem (70 CE)
- It was a public spectacle designed to terrorize the population

The scene would have been crowded, chaotic, unremarkable to the average passerby who had seen it a hundred times before.

## The Stake, Not the Cross

Known for a fact: crucifixions were carried out on torture stakes - vertical poles where arms were held above the head so breathing became difficult.

The traditional cross shape - the T or the crucifix - may not be accurate.

**The Greek Word Stauros**:
The Greek word "stauros" (σταυρός) means stake, pole, or upright timber. The Liddell-Scott Greek-English Lexicon (the standard academic reference) defines it as "an upright pale or stake."

The crossbeam (patibulum) was sometimes added, but this wasn't universal. Many victims died on simple vertical poles.

**The Pagan Cross**:
The cross symbol predates Christianity by millennia:
- The Egyptian ankh (☥) - the symbol of life
- The Babylonian cross of Tammuz
- The Hindu swastika (originally a solar symbol)
- Various solar crosses in pre-Christian Europe

When Constantine merged Christianity with Roman imperialism, which symbol did he choose? The torture stake of the actual execution? Or the pre-existing pagan cross already embedded in Roman consciousness?

## The Dual Crucifixion Theory

Here is the theory - not proven, but worth considering:

Rome understood that Yahusha could be a threat. Not because he commanded armies. Not because he had massive crowds. The threat was spiritual. The threat was to the control system.

If Rome perceived this threat, consider two scenarios:

**Scenario One: Political Theater**
A public crucifixion was needed - for the crowd, for the record, for the spectacle. But what if a substitute was used? A puppet for the show, while the actual Yahusha was quietly executed elsewhere?

This would explain the existence of two tomb sites in Jerusalem, each claimed as authentic:
- The Church of the Holy Sepulchre - the ornate, official site
- The Garden Tomb - a more modest, family-oriented tomb also claimed as authentic

If there were two crucifixions - one public theater, one private execution - there might be two burials.

**Scenario Two: Historical Revision**
The Gospels weren't written until approximately 40-70 years after the events. That's a significant gap. Long enough for Rome to influence what was recorded. Long enough to change the name. Long enough to alter the narrative.

Consider what Rome needed: a version of the story they could control. A messiah whose name didn't contain the power of Yahuah. A religion that could be merged with existing pagan structures.

## The Name Switch

If the name was changed from Yahusha to Jesus, what is actually being invoked when that name is called?

Everything is frequency. Everything is vibration.

The name Yahusha contains the frequency signature of Yahuah - "Yahuah is salvation." It connects to the Father.

# CHAPTER 20: THE KING JAMES CORRUPTION

The King James Bible is a beautiful work of literature. Its cadences are unmatched. Its phrases have shaped the English language. Its influence on Western culture is immeasurable.

But it is also a product of its time. And that time was one of political compromise, religious conflict, and intentional obfuscation.

## The Historical Context

**King James I of England** (reigned 1603-1625):
- Believed in the divine right of kings
- Commissioned the translation partly to counter the Geneva Bible's anti-monarchical notes
- Gave specific instructions to the translators about how certain terms should be rendered

**The Translators' Instructions**:
King James gave fifteen rules to the translators, including:
- The old ecclesiastical words were to be kept (church, baptism, etc.)
- No marginal notes except for explaining Hebrew and Greek words
- The Bishops' Bible was to be followed unless the original languages required change

These weren't neutral scholarly instructions. They were political guidelines that ensured certain doctrines and institutional structures were preserved.

## The Substitutions

The most significant substitution was the removal of the divine name.

**From Hebrew to English - the journey of substitution**:

1. **Hebrew**: YHWH (יהוה) - the divine name, spoken as Yahuah
2. **Septuagint (early)**: YHWH preserved in Hebrew letters
3. **Septuagint (later)**: Kyrios (Lord) substituted
4. **Latin Vulgate**: Dominus (Lord)
5. **King James**: LORD (in small capitals)

Each step introduced additional changes. The cumulative effect was a text that still contains truth but is wrapped in layers of substitution.

**Other Key Substitutions**:

| Hebrew/Greek | KJV Translation | Issues |
|--------------|-----------------|--------|
| Elohim | God | "God" derives from pagan deity names |
| Adon/Kyrios | Lord | Generic title replacing the name |
| Mashiach | Christ | Greek adaptation of Hebrew "Anointed One" |
| Yahusha | Jesus | Name meaning lost entirely |
| Ecclesia | Church | "Church" from "Circe" (Greek goddess) vs. "assembly/congregation" |
| Sabbatou | Sabbath | Sometimes inconsistently translated |

## The Translation vs. Interpretation Problem

Translation involves choices. Some passages can be legitimately rendered different ways. But the KJV translators often made interpretive choices that served doctrinal agendas:

**Example: Acts 12:4**
KJV: "And when he had apprehended him, he put him in prison... intending after **Easter** to bring him forth to the people."

The Greek word is "pascha" (πάσχα) - Passover. Every other occurrence in the KJV is translated "Passover." But here, it becomes "Easter" - a pagan fertility festival named after the goddess Ishtar/Eostre.

Why this one exception? Because the translators wanted to legitimize Easter observance.

**Example: Matthew 28:1**
KJV: "In the end of the sabbath, as it began to dawn toward the first day of the week..."

The Greek reads: "Opse de sabbaton, te epiphoskouse eis mian sabbaton" - "After the Sabbaths, as it was getting light toward the first of the Sabbaths..."

The word "sabbaton" (σαββάτων) appears twice but is translated differently - once as "sabbath," once as "week." This obscures the timing of events.

## The Restoration Option

This isn't about abandoning the Bible. It's about reading with awareness. Understanding that the text you hold has been through many hands.

**Tools for Recovery**:
- **Strong's Concordance**: Maps English words to Hebrew/Greek originals
- **Interlinear Bibles**: Show original language alongside English
- **The Cepher**: Restores names and includes removed books
- **Hebrew/Greek Lexicons**: Provide word-by-word meaning
- **Dead Sea Scrolls**: Preserve pre-Masoretic Hebrew texts

The goal: Seek the original wherever possible. Not to abandon scripture, but to strip away the layers of substitution and find what was actually said.

# CHAPTER 22: THE CALENDAR AND THE FESTIVALS

The modern calendar used worldwide is the Gregorian calendar, implemented by Pope Gregory XIII in 1582. But before examining what was changed, consider what was original.

## The Biblical Calendar

Scripture establishes a lunar-solar calendar:
- Months begin with the new moon (Tehilliym/Psalm 81:3)
- The year begins in spring with Aviv/Nisan (Shemoth/Exodus 12:2)
- The Sabbath is the seventh day, not Sunday
- Feast days are set by Yahuah, not by human decree

**The Original Festivals**:

| Festival | Hebrew Name | Timing | Significance |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------------|
| Passover | Pesach | 14th of Aviv | Redemption from Egypt |
| Unleavened Bread | Chag HaMatzot | 15th-21st of Aviv | Sanctification |
| Firstfruits | Bikkurim | Day after Sabbath during Unleavened Bread | Resurrection |
| Weeks/Pentecost | Shavuot | 50 days after Firstfruits | Torah given, Spirit given |
| Trumpets | Yom Teruah | 1st of 7th month | Awakening/ingathering |
| Day of Atonement | Yom Kippur | 10th of 7th month | Judgment/covering |
| Tabernacles | Sukkot | 15th-22nd of 7th month | Dwelling with Yahuah |

## The Substitutions

**Sabbath → Sunday**:
The seventh day Sabbath (Saturday) was replaced with Sunday worship:
- Emperor Constantine decreed Sunday as the official day of rest in 321 CE
- The Council of Laodicea (363-364 CE) condemned "Judaizing" by resting on Sabbath

**Passover → Easter**:
Passover was replaced with Easter:
- The Council of Nicaea (325 CE) decreed Easter should never coincide with Passover
- Easter timing was set by solar calculations, not biblical months
- The name "Easter" derives from the goddess Eostre/Ishtar

**Sukkot → Christmas**:
The Feast of Tabernacles was replaced with Christmas:
- December 25th was the birthday of Sol Invictus (the Roman sun god)
- Also celebrated as Saturnalia
- The birth of Yahusha was more likely during Sukkot (based on Zechariah's priestly service)

**The Effect**:
Each substitution disconnected believers from the original appointments (mo'edim) that Yahuah established. The replacement holidays align with pagan festivals, not biblical ones.
